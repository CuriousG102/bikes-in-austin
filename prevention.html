Bike Loss Prevention

Bike Locking Techniques

Bike Not Locked Up

Low Difficulty

This is the easiest way to get your bike stolen. More than 50% of bikes are stolen that are not even locked up. Even if you live on the top floor of your apartment it is still important to lock up your bike.

Bike with Cable Lock

Medium Difficulty

The process to cut a cable lock includes using a set of bolt cutters and itâ€™s pretty easy for the common criminal to get your bike. The recommendation is to not buy this type of lock but if must then get a thick one with interwoven threads and tie it around a thick tree.

Bike Locked with a U-Lock

High Difficulty

Utilize a U-lock that physically locks both ends of the U to the cross-bar. Cheaper and older U-locks can be broken into without any tools and a simple pen pressed into the locking mechanism.

Bike Locked with Two Locks For Your Bike

Very High Difficulty

This is the best practice on how to properly and safely lock your bike. Everything that could be easily removed is secured: the U-lock secures the frame and one wheel to the pole, and the cable secures the second wheel to the U-lock.

Location of Your Bike

High Crime Area

Utilizing the map of the Austin Police Department Incident reports allows you to identify the locations in the Austin area where most bike crimes occur. The recommendation is to not lock up your bike in that area.

Where You Park Your Bike Matters

Even if you live on the second floor of an apartment you still need to lock up your bike. Make sure that it is locked up in a well lighted area.

Time of Day

The recommendation is to never leave your bike overnight.
